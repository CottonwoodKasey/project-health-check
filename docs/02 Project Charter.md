# Project Health Check - Project Charter

## 1. Project Purpose

Project Health Check is based on the principle that people are the foundation of successful projects. When workers have the capacity, support, and conditions needed to do their work well, organizations are better able to achieve their goals.

However, project challenges such as unrealistic workloads, unclear priorities, scope creep, resource constraints, and communication failures can create conditions that contribute to stress, burnout, and reduced project performance.

Project Health Check aims to create an organizational learning platform that uses worker experiences as an important source of insight into project health. Through continuous feedback loops, the platform helps organizations identify emerging challenges, address risks earlier, and improve both project outcomes and workplace wellbeing.

The platform is designed not only to identify challenges but also to support organizations in testing potential improvements and learning from the results.

## Guiding Principles

### Shared Agency

Project Health Check is designed to support learning and improvement throughout the organization. Insights generated by the platform should empower individuals, teams, and those responsible for organizational decisions to identify opportunities, test changes, and contribute to better ways of working.

### Improvement Through Experimentation

Project Health Check helps organizations learn by supporting small, intentional changes and capturing what happens afterward.

### Contextual Understanding

Project Health Check recognizes that worker experiences and project outcomes are shaped by interactions among organizational conditions, workload, teams, projects, and individual circumstances. Meaningful insights require understanding these contexts rather than interpreting individual indicators in isolation.

## 2. Project Rationale

Projects are complex systems where human factors and project factors interact. Workload, communication, resources, psychological safety, and other workplace conditions influence how effectively teams can achieve project objectives.

By collecting information about worker experiences alongside project health indicators, Project Health Check aims to identify patterns, relationships, and emerging risks that may help organizations better understand the conditions under which work occurs.

The platform recognizes that organizational outcomes emerge from interactions among multiple factors rather than from any single indicator in isolation. Rather than functioning as a diagnostic or evaluation tool, Project Health Check is designed as a learning system that helps organizations identify patterns, test potential improvements, and evaluate whether changes improve both project conditions and worker experiences.

## 3. Project Objectives

### Objective 1: Develop a prototype organizational learning platform

Design and develop a prototype platform that enables organizations to collect structured feedback about project conditions, worker experiences, and organizational factors.

### Objective 2: Explore patterns and relationships among project and human factors

Develop approaches for organizing and analyzing project health and worker experience data to identify emerging patterns, potential risks, and opportunities for improvement.

### Objective 3: Support continuous improvement through experimentation

Enable individuals, teams, and organizational leaders to identify opportunities for improvement, test changes, document approaches, and evaluate whether those changes improve project conditions and worker experiences.

### Objective 4: Prioritize user benefit and psychological safety

Design the platform so that participation provides value to contributors while protecting confidentiality and encouraging honest feedback.

### Objective 5: Evaluate whether the platform generates actionable insights

Pilot the platform with users and assess whether it provides meaningful insights that help individuals, teams, and organizations improve how work is planned, supported, and delivered.

## 4. Project Scope

### Included

The initial Project Health Check prototype will include:

- A system for collecting recurring feedback about project conditions, worker experiences, and organizational factors.
- The ability for individuals to provide feedback while maintaining confidentiality and psychological safety.
- Aggregated reporting that helps users identify patterns, trends, and potential areas of concern.
- A framework for connecting human factors and project factors to better understand relationships between them.
- An improvement experiment feature that allows individuals, teams, and organizations to identify strategies, test changes, document implementation, and reflect on outcomes.
- A mechanism for capturing lessons learned and organizational knowledge from improvement experiments.

### Excluded

The initial prototype will not include:

- Individual employee performance evaluation or ranking.
- Automated decisions about individuals, teams, or projects.
- Fully automated selection or implementation of recommendations or interventions.
- Predictive models requiring large historical datasets.
- A replacement for existing project management systems.
- Organization-wide benchmarking across unrelated organizations.
