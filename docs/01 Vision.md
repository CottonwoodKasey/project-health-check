# Project Health Check - Vision Document

## 1. Vision Statement

Project Health Check is a platform designed to help organizations improve project success by creating continuous feedback loops between staff, teams, and leadership. It helps organizations identify emerging challenges early, learn from experience, and build healthier working environments where people and projects can thrive.

## 2. The Problem

Many organizations struggle to identify problems until they become serious. Project delays, communication failures, burnout, staff turnover, and repeated mistakes often emerge gradually but remain invisible to decision-makers.

Although organizations frequently conduct reviews, surveys, and lessons-learned exercises, these processes are often inconsistent, occur too late, and do not create meaningful change.

## 3. Why Current Solutions Fall Short

Existing approaches often focus on one part of the problem:

- Employee engagement surveys measure satisfaction but may not connect results to specific projects.
- Project management tools track tasks and deadlines but may not capture human and organizational factors.
- Lessons-learned processes often happen at the end of projects, when opportunities to adapt have already passed.

Organizations need a way to continuously understand both project performance and the experiences of the people doing the work.

## 4. Our Vision

Project Health Check will create a continuous learning system where staff experience, project information, and organizational knowledge are transformed into actionable insights.

By creating regular opportunities for reflection and feedback, organizations can identify risks earlier, support staff wellbeing, and improve future decision-making.

## 5. Who We Serve

Project Health Check is designed for organizations that manage complex projects and want to improve:

- Project outcomes
- Team communication
- Staff wellbeing
- Organizational learning
- Continuous improvement

Potential users include non-profits, Indigenous organizations, government agencies, consulting firms, and other project-based organizations.

## 6. Core Principles

Project Health Check will be guided by the following principles:

### People benefit from participation
Staff should receive value from contributing feedback, not simply provide data for management.

### Psychological safety matters
People need ways to identify concerns without fear of negative consequences.

### Learning should be continuous
Organizations should capture and apply lessons during projects, not only after problems occur.

### Data should support improvement
Information should be used to identify patterns and opportunities, not assign blame.

## 7. Measures of Success

Project Health Check will be successful if it helps organizations:

- Identify project risks earlier
- Reduce repeated mistakes
- Improve communication between teams and leadership
- Support staff wellbeing
- Create stronger learning cultures

## 8. Long-Term Vision

In the future, Project Health Check will become an intelligent organizational learning platform that helps teams understand project health in real time and provides guidance for improving outcomes.

It will help organizations move from reacting to problems toward continuously learning and adapting.

## 9. Intended Benefits

Project Health Check supports everyone who contributes to an organization’s work by creating a continuous learning loop where experiences, challenges, and insights are shared, understood, and transformed into collective knowledge and meaningful improvements.
